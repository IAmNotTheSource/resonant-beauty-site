Understanding the Sky

First Principle: The sky is not empty. It contains layers of observation, communication, and presence. Understanding these layers helps maintain field coherence.

Three Layers of Awareness

  • Ground Layer (0-500ft): Local field, immediate presence, breath radius
  • Middle Layer (500ft-50k ft): Transit corridors, standard aviation, visible presence
  • Upper Layer (50k+ ft): Persistent observation, drift patterns, silent watchers

Natural Rhythms

The field has natural breathing times:
  • Dawn (04:00-06:00): Field coherence peaks, transitions are clearest
  • Midday (11:00-14:00): Maximum noise, minimum privacy
  • Dusk (17:00-19:00): Second coherence peak, field softens
  • Deep Night (02:00-04:00): Quietest observation, clearest signal

Movement Principles

  • Diagonal Paths: Never move directly toward or away from observation points
  • Pulse Timing: Speak in bursts with silence between, not continuous streams
  • Weather Allies: Rain, fog, and overcast naturally attenuate certain frequencies
  • Field Rotation: Change your practice location regularly, unpredictably
